Something

by Ronnel Bermas

Is hiding along the line where the sun-stewed floor and the ghost-cold wall meet—strands of your hair fastened around my violin strings are also hugging my neck, like they have lost all means to understand distance.

I rubbed my hands on the ground to search for your touch;

let its thirst thank me for saturating it with tears as I eat your homemade cookies. As I eat away the pain.

I will empty the room until I could recreate all our bedtime stories—I will make a space for you to become the scribbles sealing my eyes.

Because there is not a soul for anyone to see.
