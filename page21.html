You

by Ronnel Bermas

I am under the blankets, wide enough
To cover your absence,
Keeping everything to myself—
The ruins of a body broken beyond anger
From having too much to keep
because by the time I finish this
I've already remembered what makes thirsty leaves
Lust for the soil
be there, forevermore.

I am under the impression
That I will never run out of hugs
That I can piece you together
From the shards of a heart
Unable to contain its pulse
For what are hugs
If not to confirm
that we're alive—
that we refuse to let go of ourselves
And finish this poem

Because by then, you would have begun
And I would have ended
And by having less of me, there would be more
Of love.
