Lie

by Ronnel Bermas

The dim silence of the halls emanates from the sides of the door; despite the embellishment of wallpapers—everything swears to be still. I can feel myself flatly pushing my back against the duvet, as though to grout an abyss that mourns for a loss.

       But I couldn't cry at all.
       I could only remember.

The stillness diminishes in retrograde as she gets closer, reappearing as puzzle pieces of shadows and sounds I will never recognize again—the more I piece them together, the more pieces become lost.

      The night dominates; my eyes surrender.

      And I lie

      myself to sleep.
